Chronic Kidney Disease Prediction Risk Prediction

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a condition characterized by a gradual loss of kidney function over time. It can lead to kidney failure and the need for dialysis or a kidney transplant. Early detection is crucial for slowing the progression and improving patient outcomes.

Key Risk Factors
  • Diabetes: High blood sugar levels can damage the kidneys over time.
  • High Blood Pressure (Hypertension): Can cause damage to kidney filters.
  • High Creatinine and Urea Levels: Indicators of poor kidney filtration.
  • Protein in Urine: A sign of kidney damage.
  • Low Hemoglobin: Often associated with chronic kidney issues.
  • Electrolyte Imbalance: Sodium and potassium level fluctuations may signal CKD.
  • Family History: A genetic predisposition may increase risk.
